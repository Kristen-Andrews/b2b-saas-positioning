## 3. Primary alternative

**What do they do today if not you?**

The primary alternative is almost never a direct competitor. It's usually:
- Spreadsheets and email
- Doing nothing
- An internal tool built by the ops team
- A legacy system nobody wants to replace
- A generalist product used sideways (Notion, Airtable, Google Sheets)

**Their current workflow:**
> Describe it in their words. Two sentences maximum.

**Why it persists:**
> What's the friction that keeps them stuck? Cost of change? Fear? Habit? Lack of budget?

**What breaks first when they scale:**
> The failure mode that pushes them to look.

**Stress test:** If you replaced "spreadsheets" with your primary alternative in the sentence "They're leaving spreadsheets because...", does it still ring true?

## 5. Value proof

**One receipt per attribute.**

Each unique attribute above must have a proof point. Not adjectives. Evidence.

| Attribute | Proof point (metric, named customer, or documented capability) |

| 1 | |
| 2 | |
| 3 | |
| 4 | |
| 5 | |

**Types of proof, ranked by strength:**

1. Named customer + specific metric ("Acme Corp cut audit prep from 6 weeks to 4 days")
2. Aggregate metric across customer base ("Average time-to-value: 14 days")
3. Documented capability with a link ("Native integration with [system], see docs")
4. Third-party validation (analyst report, industry certification)
5. Expert credential ("Team includes 3 former [respected org] engineers")

**Stress test:** If a prospect asks "prove it" for each attribute, can you answer in one sentence with a fact? If you have to describe rather than cite, it's not proof yet.

## 6. Enemy

**What status quo are you fighting?**

Not a competitor. A condition. The thing your ICP shouldn't accept anymore.

**The old way:**
> One sentence describing the status quo your category exists to end.

**Why it's broken now:**
> What changed? New regulation? New scale? New buyer expectation? New technology?

**The cost of staying stuck:**
> What does the ICP lose by not changing? Time, money, risk, opportunity?

**The new way (your category):**
> One sentence describing the world you're building.

**Stress test:** Would your best customer nod along to your enemy statement without you in the room? If they'd push back or shrug, you haven't found the right enemy yet.
